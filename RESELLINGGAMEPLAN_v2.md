# Reselling Gameplan v2 — Replace $4k/mo Amazon Warehouse Income

**Your profile:** $200 starting capital · no time limit · 10–20 hrs/week (still working the warehouse) · good big-box store access · no clothes/shoes · **NO used electronics from strangers** (no scam, stolen-goods, or broken-item risk — you're not a pawn shop).

**Version history:** v1 assumed $500–$1,000 and leaned on used Apple/console flips. v2 (this file) reflects the $200 budget, no deadline, and a new-items-first risk profile, adjusted after researching top reselling guides (eBay beginner bestsellers, *Flip It!*), business-building classics (*The E-Myth Revisited*, *Profit First*, *The $100 Startup*), and 2026 market data on liquidation/pallet flipping.

---

## The core strategy (why it's built this way)

- **Online-first, list-driven sourcing.** You don't wander stores hunting. `/product-scout` delivers a ranked buy-list from online sources — mispriced eBay listings (bad titles/photos = underpriced, relist optimized), Facebook Marketplace shipped deals, online clearance (Walmart.com, Target.com, Woot, Ollie's online), book arbitrage (BookScouter-style price gaps), and bundle/lot opportunities. **In-store trips happen ONLY when the list names a specific item, a specific store, and a max price.** You execute the list; the system does the finding.
- **Mostly new, some used where it's easy.** New/sealed is the default. Used is fine where condition is simple and low-risk: books, media, board games with complete-parts photos. Used electronics stay ⚠️ RISK.
- **Sell on two platforms:** **eBay** (shipped, ~13.25% + $0.30/order) + **Facebook Marketplace** (local, 0% fees) for anything bulky or locally hot. New eBay accounts are capped ~10 items/$500 per month with payout holds — book/consumable volume is what seasons the account and raises limits.
- **Buy rule (never break it):** expected sale price ≥ **3× cost** for items under $10, ≥ **2.5×** above that. Minimum **$8 net** per book/media flip, **$15 net** per retail-arbitrage flip.
- **Profit First rule (new in v2):** from every sale, **10% goes to a "pay yourself" pot and 15% to a tax pot** before anything is reinvested. The rest compounds into inventory. This keeps the business honest and sustainable from day one.
- **No-deadline advantage:** you never have to force a marginal buy. If a sourcing run finds nothing that clears the 3× rule, buying nothing is a win.

## Your 3 focus categories (v2)

### 1. Books & media — the new profit floor (replaces used Apple)
Textbooks (highest value — science/medical/nursing), niche non-fiction, first editions, out-of-print titles, video game strategy guides, box sets, CDs/vinyl with collector demand.
**Why:** cheapest sourcing in all of reselling ($0.25–$2 at library sales, thrift stores, garage sales), nothing can be stolen or broken, media-mail shipping is the cheapest USPS class, and documented flippers run this to $4k+/mo. Textbooks spike every August/September and January.
**Tool:** scan barcodes with a free app or check eBay sold comps before buying. If a book doesn't show $10+ net, leave it.
**Target: $8–$40 net per flip, occasional $75+ textbook hits.**

### 2. Clearance consumables & discontinued items — the velocity engine
Razor cartridges, printer ink, discontinued cosmetics/hair color, niche snacks, baby-care items — Walmart/Target/Ollie's clearance endcaps.
**Why:** fastest sell-through on eBay, repeat buyers, no sizing issues, $5–15/unit fits a $200 budget, and discontinued items regularly sell 2–10× retail. This fills your eBay quota and builds feedback fast.
**Target: $10–$25 net per flip.**

### 3. New/sealed toys, games & accessories — the margin lane
Clearance LEGO (retired sets appreciate), sealed board games, new-in-box OEM game controllers and accessories, seasonal toy clearance (January is gold), trading-card sealed product ONLY at clearance prices.
**Why:** toys/gaming carry some of the highest eBay sell-through rates, everything is new/sealed so v1's "test for stick drift" problem disappears, and retired sets have a built-in appreciation curve.
**Target: $15–$50 net per flip.**

### 4. Electronics — allowed, but ALWAYS flagged ⚠️ RISK
Electronics are not banned — they're just never treated as a normal buy. Every electronics item, anywhere in the system (scout lists, appraisals, listings, inventory.csv), carries a **⚠️ RISK** label and stricter rules:
- **Prefer new/sealed or trusted-source** (store clearance, open-box from retailers with receipts, trade-in programs) over stranger meetups.
- **If used from an individual:** IMEI/serial check, activation-lock check, and power-on test are mandatory — no exceptions, or PASS.
- **Higher bar:** minimum **$50 net** expected and never more than 20% of working capital in a single electronics buy.
- **`/make-listing` requirement:** electronics listings are generated with a RISK header for you (internal note, not shown to buyers), extra condition-disclosure language, serial-number photo reminder, and signature-confirmation shipping above $100.
Electronics are the highest-margin lane — this framework lets you take the occasional great one without becoming a pawn shop.

**Also deferred:** small manifested liquidation lots (<$150, local pickup, manifest verified) become an option around month 3–4. Not required to hit $4k.

## Phases (no deadlines — gated by milestones, not dates)

### Phase 0 — Setup
eBay seller account + clean FB Marketplace profile · order free USPS boxes · start `inventory.csv` from purchase #1 (1099-K rules) · install a barcode-scanning/comps workflow.

### Phase 1 — First $200 → $400 (validate)
2 online sourcing sessions/week: run `/product-scout` for each lane, buy only what's on the list, plus any specific in-store pickups it names (item + store + max price). List everything within 24h of arrival. 10 best items to eBay, rest to FB local.
**Gate to Phase 2: capital doubled AND 10+ eBay feedback.**

### Phase 2 — $400 → $1,000 (systemize)
eBay limits rise with feedback → shift more volume to eBay. Run `/weekly-review` + `/next-week` every Sunday. Start Profit First splits. Add textbook seasonality plays.
**Gate to Phase 3: ~$1,000 working capital AND a full month ≥ $500 net.**

### Phase 3 — $1,000+ (scale to $4k/mo)
Target monthly mix:
- ~60 book/media flips × $15 avg net = **$900**
- ~50 consumable sales × $15 net = **$750**
- ~45 toy/game/accessory flips × $30 net = **$1,350**
- ~10 higher-ticket finds (retired LEGO, textbook spikes, optional trusted-source electronics) × $100 net = **$1,000**
- **≈ $4,000/mo net**, ~15–20 hrs/week.

**Rule (unchanged): don't quit the warehouse until 2 consecutive months ≥ $4k profit.**
**Honest note:** this mix needs ~165 sales/mo vs v1's ~95, because per-item margins are lower without used electronics. That's ~5–6 shipments/day — real work, but proven doable at 15–20 hrs/week with same-day listing habits and batch shipping. The trade is deliberate: lower margin per item, near-zero fraud/condition risk, higher consistency.

## The automation (Claude skills — now 5)

1. **`/product-scout <category> <budget>`** — ranked buy-list from live sold comps + clearance/deal data. Enforces the 3×/2.5× rule.
2. **`/appraise <item> <price> <condition>`** — BUY/PASS + max offer from live eBay sold comps and fee math. (Now used mostly in-store on clearance finds and at book sales, not meetups.)
3. **`/make-listing <item details>`** — keyword-optimized eBay title/description/specifics + FB version, copy-paste ready. **Auto-detects electronics and prepends a ⚠️ RISK block** (internal checklist: serial photographed, locks checked, tested, signature confirmation if >$100) plus tightened condition-disclosure language in the listing itself.
4. **`/weekly-review`** — reads `inventory.csv`: P&L, sell-through, aging inventory (markdown suggestions at 21 days), Profit First splits, progress vs milestone gates.
5. **`/next-week`** *(new)* — takes `/weekly-review` output and produces next week's action plan: goals for YOU, tasks for CLAUDE, one process improvement (*E-Myth*: work ON the business) — **and a scaling ladder**: once data supports it, it actively pushes average net-per-sale upward ($10–15 → $25 → $50 → $100+ tiers) by recommending higher-ticket lanes (retired LEGO, textbook seasons, sealed collectibles, ⚠️ RISK electronics, manifested lots) so revenue grows from bigger flips, not just more hours.

All skills read/write **`inventory.csv`** (date bought, source, item, category, cost, listed price, platform, sold date, sold price, fees, shipping, net profit) as the single source of truth. Firecrawl remains the comps/deal-data engine behind `/product-scout` and `/appraise`.

## Listing Optimization Playbook (baked into /make-listing — updated from 2026 research)

eBay's search algorithm (Cassini) ranks on relevance + performance + buyer experience. Every listing the system generates follows this checklist, and the checklist itself gets updated as `/next-week` researches new findings:

1. **Title (80 chars):** most important keywords FIRST — brand, model, size, color, condition. Build from eBay's autocomplete (real buyer searches). No filler words, no keyword stuffing.
2. **Item specifics: fill EVERY field, not just required ones.** In 2026 specifics are arguably more important than titles — they power sidebar filters, and unfilled fields make you invisible to filtered searches.
3. **All 12 photo slots.** Clean first photo on neutral background (it's the search thumbnail). For used items: photograph every flaw — it prevents returns and builds trust.
4. **Price to sold comps, not asking prices.** Enable Best Offer with auto-accept/auto-decline thresholds. Charm pricing ($24.99 not $25).
5. **Free shipping when margin allows** (bake it into price) — captures the free-shipping filter audience and a ranking boost.
6. **Ship within 1 business day, offer 30-day returns** — both are tracked ranking signals.
7. **Bundles & lots:** 5 slow $5 items become one $25 listing — one shipment, higher ticket, feeds the scaling ladder.
8. **Promoted Listings: start at 2%, not eBay's suggested 10–15%** — only for new listings without sales history or high-margin items.
9. **Mobile check:** 60%+ of traffic is mobile — preview every description on a phone.

**The improvement loop (never "set and forget"):** every week, `/weekly-review` diagnoses listings — **low impressions = weak title/keywords → rewrite; views but no sales = price/photos/trust → adjust; sold fast = raise price on the next one.** `/next-week` assigns Claude one listing-research task per week (new Cassini changes, category trends, competitor title analysis) so the playbook keeps improving forever.

## Principles adopted from the research

- **Build a system, not a job** (*E-Myth*): every recurring decision becomes a skill or a checklist. If you did it manually twice, automate or document it.
- **Profit First**: take profit and tax off the top of every sale. A business that never pays you isn't a business.
- **Start tiny, validate with sales** (*$100 Startup*): $200 is enough. Real sold data beats any projection — the weekly review is the only forecast that matters.
- **Sell-through rate > margin** (eBay bestseller guides): a 40% margin that sells in 5 days beats a 70% margin that sits 90 days. `/weekly-review` tracks days-to-sale per category and shifts buying toward what moves.

## Risks to respect (v2)
Payout holds (keep the $30 reserve, growing to $250 by Phase 3) · FB local payment scams (cash or tap-to-pay only, meet in public) · returns (photograph everything before shipping) · capital discipline (never >20% of capital in one item) · pallet FOMO (no unmanifested pallets, ever — most beginners lose money on their first ones).
